1. What makes A‑SAFE barriers different from steel barriers?
A‑SAFE barriers are made from advanced polymer technology that flexes and absorbs impact energy. Unlike steel, which transfers impact to the floor and often needs replacing after a collision, A‑SAFE barriers self-recover, protecting people, equipment, floors, and vehicles while reducing long-term costs.
2. What does PAS 13 crash testing mean?
PAS 13 is an internationally recognized code of practice that defines how barriers should be tested and installed. A‑SAFE barriers are independently crash-tested to these rigorous standards, so you can trust they perform exactly as promised when it matters most.
3. How do I know which barrier strength I need?
We assess your site for factors such as vehicle weight, speed, and traffic flow. From this, we recommend a barrier system engineered to absorb the specific impact forces in your facility. We can also share some indicative potential impact forces based upon your vehicle details when input into our impact calculator.
4. Do A‑SAFE barriers stop forklifts completely?
They are designed to absorb energy and slow vehicles safely while protecting structures and people. Depending on the impact conditions, the barrier can stop or significantly decelerate a vehicle, preventing serious damage or injury.
5. Can barriers protect against racking damage?
Yes. Specially designed racking protectors and low-level barriers shield racking legs and structures from forklift impacts, which are one of the most common causes of warehouse damage and racking collapse.
6. How long does installation take?
It really depends on site size and complexity. Our teams work around your operations to minimize downtime and disruption and it is typically a lot faster than steel alternative barriers due to A-SAFE modular assembly and supplied, fit-for-purpose floor fixings.
7. Are A‑SAFE barriers maintenance-free?
Unlike steel barriers, A‑SAFE barriers don’t rust, need repainting, or warp after impact. Periodic visual checks are all that’s needed, but we can also provide periodic inspections or training for your teams on site.
8. What industries are A‑SAFE barriers suitable for?
Our barriers are used across logistics, food & drink, automotive, airports, manufacturing, and pharmaceuticals. Any industry that values the protection of people, assets, and infrastructure can benefit from A‑SAFE systems.
9. What’s the lifespan of a polymer barrier?
A‑SAFE barriers typically last years longer than steel alternatives because they absorb impacts rather than bend or break. They retain their strength and appearance even in demanding environments.
10. How do barriers affect warehouse traffic flow?
Properly planned barrier systems guide safe vehicle and pedestrian movement, making facilities more organized. This reduces congestion, improves flow, and minimizes near-misses.
11. Do you offer on-site safety assessments?
Yes. Our expert team will visit your site, review traffic and pedestrian risks, and provide a customized safety plan. This service is free of charge and comes with a detailed proposal.
12. Can barriers be moved if my layout changes?
Yes. A‑SAFE barriers are modular and can be relocated or reconfigured as your facility changes, making them a flexible long-term investment.
13. Are barriers resistant to chemicals, oils, and weather?
Our advanced polymers are resistant to most chemicals, oils, and fuels. Outdoor barrier solutions also include UV stabilizers to withstand weather without fading or degrading.
14. Do A‑SAFE barriers comply with OSHA and EU regulations?
Yes. A‑SAFE barriers are designed and installed in compliance with international safety standards, including OSHA requirements and EU directives for workplace safety.
15. Do you provide CAD drawings for planning?
Yes. We create detailed CAD layouts to help you visualize barrier placement and ensure your system integrates seamlessly with your facility.
16. How do barriers help with insurance compliance?
Properly installed, crash-tested barriers reduce risks, which can support compliance with insurer requirements and in some cases help reduce premiums.
17. Can barriers prevent vehicle downtime costs?
Yes. Barriers absorb impacts to protect vehicles from major structural damage, which reduces repair costs, downtime, and productivity losses.

Yes. When pedestrians and operators see clear segregation and protection, it reduces anxiety. This leads to a calmer, more confident workforce and improved productivity.

Absolutely. We offer barriers and bollards specifically designed to shield machinery, control panels, conveyors, columns, and even building walls from impact damage.
26. Are there options for outdoor use (loading bays, car parks)?
Yes. A‑SAFE provides outdoor‑rated barriers and bollards with UV protection and weather-resistant finishes. These are ideal for truck yards, car parks, and external walkways.
27. What are the environmental benefits of polymer barriers?
Our barriers are 100% recyclable and have a longer lifespan than steel, which reduces material waste. Lower replacement frequency also means fewer resources consumed over the life of your system.
28. How do I know when it’s time to replace a barrier?
Polymer barriers are highly durable and self‑recover after impact, but if a barrier has sustained a major hit, it should be inspected. We provide clear guidelines and can offer inspections to confirm integrity.
29. Can I start with a small project and expand later?

31. Can A‑SAFE barriers be used in automated warehouses or with AGVs (Automated Guided Vehicles)?
Yes. Our barriers are designed to guide and protect both manned and unmanned traffic. In highly automated environments, they help define pathways for AGVs and protect robotics, storage systems, and segregate personnel zones to not interfere or disrupt AGV traffic flow.
32. What happens if an A‑SAFE barrier is hit multiple times?
Our unique polymer design absorbs impacts and flexes back into shape. Repeated minor impacts typically cause no damage. After severe or repeated collisions in the same spot, the barrier can be inspected and individual parts replaced as and when may be necessary.

Proper impact protection helps companies comply with health and safety regulations, reducing the risk of fines, legal claims, and insurance disputes after incidents.

36. Are there lightweight options for low-speed areas?
Yes. We offer a range of barrier types, from lightweight pedestrian guides for low-speed areas to heavy-duty systems for high-traffic, high-impact zones.
37. What materials are A‑SAFE barriers made from?
Our barriers are made from patented advanced polymer blends called Memaplex™ and Monoplex™, specifically engineered to flex, absorb energy, and return to shape after impact.
38. Can barriers be installed without drilling into floors?
In some cases, temporary or surface-mounted solutions can be provided. However, for maximum impact protection, anchoring barriers to the floor ensures full crash-tested performance. We also have special slider base plates to allow for full impact performance along with the ability to easily remove barriers without any tools e.g. for periodic maintenance etc.
39. Do barriers make forklift drivers overconfident?
No. In fact, clear separation of vehicles and pedestrians reduces driver stress and increases attentiveness. Barriers are a safeguard, not a substitute for safe driving practices.
